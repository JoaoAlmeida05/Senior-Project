A Senior Project
By Joao Almeida

Joao was born in Orlando, FL and moved to Brazil when he was still little. He was raised by his family over there and after 14 years decided to live in California and challenge himself. Always seeking to develop new skills, Joao believes that everything in life could be done in a better way if we add a little more effort to it.

Precis for “Injuries in Brazilian Jiujitsu Prompt Introspection in Growing Martial Art”:
In John Wiegand’s essay “Injuries in Brazilian Jujitsu Prompt Introspection in Growing Martial Art” (Sep 21, 2023), he explains why people are getting injured practicing jiu-jitsu. Wiegand develops his ideas by interviewing Gracie, who is an instructor, what are the possible ways of getting safer practice, and interviewing people who used to practice and gave up on it because of the risk of injuries and not feeling safe. Using as example he tells how popular this martial art is getting by talking about famous people who started practicing it, such as Joe Rogan and Mark Zuckerberg.
Wiengand opens his essay by explaining what jiujitsu is, and closes it by showing that there are better ways of practicing it, which proves that he addresses this article to people who are interested in starting it. Precis for “Special Issue Athletes’ Performance and Analysis in Combat Sports”:
In Tadeusz Ambroszy’s essay “Special Issue Athletes’ Performance and Analysis in Combat Sports” (2023-10-18), he explains how Martial arts can change our lives for the better, causing rapid fat loss and other benefits. Ambroszy develops his ideas by citing more articles that he encountered to compare how people got better after martial art. In order to convince people that Martial art is something beneficial to people, he exaggerates by saying that it causes rapid weight loss, which in reality what causes weight loss is eating less calories than what you need to maintain your body weight. Ambroszy’s ostensible audience is people who are interested in changing their performance or losing weight, he starts by introducing martial art to the reader and finishes it by saying the benefits of it.
